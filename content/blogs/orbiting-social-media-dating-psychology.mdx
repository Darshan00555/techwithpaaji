# Orbiting: Why Your Ex Still Watches Your Stories (But Won't Text Back)

You just went through a breakup. You’ve stopped texting. You’ve stopped calling. But every time you post a photo of your Sunday brunch or a snippet of your gym workout, there they are—at the top of your "Story Viewers" list.

They aren't liking your posts. They aren't replying to your DMs. They are just... **Orbiting.**

In 2026, **Orbiting** has become the ultimate digital mind game. It’s the "new ghosting," but worse—because they haven't actually disappeared. They are still in your digital solar system, just far enough away to never touch, but close enough to stay on your mind.

---

## What is Orbiting?

Orbiting is when someone cuts off direct communication (ghosting or breaking up) but continues to engage with your social media presence. They watch every story, like the occasional photo from two years ago, or "react" with an emoji, yet they refuse to have a real conversation.

It’s a form of **digital lingering** that keeps you in a state of "what if?" 

---

## The Psychology: Why Do People Orbit?

According to relationship experts in India, people orbit for several key reasons:

1.  **FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out):** They want to keep tabs on your life to make sure they aren't missing out on your growth.
2.  **The "Backup" Strategy:** By staying in your viewer list, they remain a "soft presence" in your mind, making it easier to reappear later (a trend known as *Zombieing*).
3.  **Validation:** Seeing that you are still "there" gives them a sense of power or comfort without the effort of a relationship.
4.  **Curiosity without Commitment:** They want to see the "movie" of your life without having to pay for the ticket.

Orbiting is exhausting because it triggers "hope" where there should be "healing." Every time you see their name, your brain does a mini-analysis: *Does this mean they miss me? Are they going to text?*

## How to Handle an Orbiter

If you find yourself being orbited in 2026, you have three options:

- **The Mute Button:** If you aren't ready to block, just mute them. If you can't see them watching you, the "spell" is broken.
- **The Block & Cleanse:** If it’s affecting your sleep or focus, block them. It’s not "petty"—it’s self-care.
- **Go Private:** Limit who can see your stories to your actual inner circle.
